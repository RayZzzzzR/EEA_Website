Computer Vision and Drones 101

  1. Home
  2. Blogs

The project 'CV and Drones 101' offered by the EEA during the winter was the first time we as freshers were introduced to the the core concepts of the Electrical department. The way the projects' work was divided into live-class assignments and Home- assignments served the purpose of immediate revision and in-depth concept testing respectively. The assignments not only tested our current understanding but also familiarized us with the concepts real life applications. The mentors were available all the time, made sure we had no pending doubts and that our concepts were crystal clear. I am grateful that EEA offered us this opportunity to develop such good technical skills during our winters.

The ”enthu” of Y22s and particularly Y23s during the winter month was truly remarkable. Everyone seemed to be at the peak of comfort while staying at home, yet this kept us engaged and occupied. Following my classes, I would head to the badminton court, establishing a routine for the month. Each day was filled with lively discussions, and then an assignment, during which I learned Python and its libraries , more into the practical applications side. Exploring image processing and drone pose-estimation through aruco markers sparked thoughts about the future potential of drone industries. We later had a practical demonstration using a Tello drone, which was the cherry on the cake. In summary, this period provided a valuable platform for exchanging ideas with friends and seniors and sure it kept us warm in the chilling months of december.
